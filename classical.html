The Classical Era

1750-1820

The Classical Era oversaw the development of much simpler forms of music, inspired by the art and architecture of the ancient Greeks and Romans, which was elegant, balanced, and more simple in form and structure. Because they were imitating the ancient "classic" cultures, the musical style of this era was known as classical music.

Classical music mostly consisted of much simpler and often shorter melodies, with composers sometimes writing symmetrical question-and-answer phrases. There were also fewer ornaments than in the Baroque period, and instead of heavy texture, music consisted of a melody with a chord accompaniment, which sounded much lighter and clearer. Plus, composers put a lot more focus on the structure of their music, with great interest in phrasing, articulation, and dynamics to express contrasts and emotions. Some of the most famous composers of this time were Joseph Haydn, Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, and Ludwig Van Beethoven.
