The Baroque Era

1600-1750

The Baroque Era was the earliest period of western classical music, and it is defined by elegant melodies and harmonies representative of the artistic style of the Age of Absolutism, which dominated Europe at the time. The art and architecture consisted of fancy decorations and unusual shapes, and Baroque music was crafted in the same way. Most Baroque musicians were supported by and commissioned to create music by institutions such as monarchies, noble families, and the Catholic Church.

Baroque music was heavily ornamented and often consisted of polyphony (the performance of two different melodies at the same time) and basso continuo (a continuing bass line). Music was often played by small groups of instruments, such as string groups or chamber orchestras. Some of the most famous composers of this time were Johann Sebastian Bach, George Frederic Handel, and Antonio Vivaldi.
